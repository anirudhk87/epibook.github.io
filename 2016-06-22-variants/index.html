The EPI variants summarized

This post clarifies questions we are often asked about EPI.

Variants

First of all, there are two basic printed variants: Elements of Programming Interviews (which is in C++), and Elements of Programming Interviews in Java.

Each of these comes in two sizes: 6 in x 9 in and 7 in x 10 in. The latter uses a larger font and greater line spacing. The smaller and larger formats contain exactly the same content.

The prose surrounding code for both EPI and EPI in Java is almost identical - the programs of course are different. (Later chapters which contain language-specific content are very different.) The content of the large and small formats is identical

Amazon does a poor job of showing the variants. For your convenience, here’s the canonical list of variants.

  • Elements of Programming Interviews (C++): 6 in x 9 in format, 7 in x 10 in format
  • Elements of Programming Interviews in Java: 6 in x 9 in format, 7 in x 10 in format

Versions

People are often confused the publication dates listed for the variants, e.g., October 2012 date listed for the original 6 in x 9in EPI (C++).

EPI has changed enormously since the initial release - the first release came at commit 1200, we are now at commit 3900. On the way we’ve added and removed problems, features, and chapters (and fixed more bugs than we care to admit).

For this reason we very strongly recommend that you buy only from Amazon itself, not from resellers: the version sold by Amazon itself is always current. Resellers may be selling old stock, or even worse, pirated copies which have very poor print quality.

Navigating the Ebook

The typographic quality of iBooks and Kindle is unacceptable for a book like EPI that has sophisticated tables, drawings, equations, code, etc. (The input to these formats is HTML and the result is hideous - trust us, we’ve tried.)

Google Play is the only ebook format that will render EPI perfectly. The PDF we provide Google includes hyperlinks that allow you to click on problems in the table of contents, and referenced page numbers. However, despite multiple attempts on our part, Google Play strips these links out, making the ebook slower to navigate and search.

With Google Play you buy the book, try it out, and ask for a refund within a certain time period, and we encourage you to take advantage of this facility.
